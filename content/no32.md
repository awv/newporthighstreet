<p class="wiki-intro-text">This prominent building on Commercial Street is best known to modern residents as the long-term home of Richard Shops, but its history stretches back much further into Newport's Victorian mercantile boom.</p>

## Key tenants
* **Richard Shops** — 1949–1980s
* **Thomas & Sons** — 1892
* **W.H. Smith & Son** — 1900s

## About the building
The structure is a classic example of High Street Victorian architecture. While the ground floor was heavily modified with 1940s glass frontage for Richard Shops, the upper storeys retain their original decorative brickwork and window apertures.

## Interesting facts
The 1949 refurbishment was considered one of the most "modern" on the street at the time, featuring a 'walk-in' arcade-style window display that allowed shoppers to view the latest fashions from three different angles before even entering the store.

## Human element
Local residents often recall the grand Christmas window displays at No. 32, which were a highlight for children visiting the town centre during the mid-20th century.
