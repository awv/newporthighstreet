NO. 1 HIGH STREET

A prominent corner property that has stood as a gateway to the High Street for generations, most famously known as the site of the Heathcock Inn.

KEY TENANTS

  • Heathcock Inn (Public House) — 1833–1844
  • John Church (Innkeeper) — 1841

ABOUT THE BUILDING

This sturdy Victorian structure features a classic corner entrance designed to capture foot traffic from both the High Street and the adjacent lanes.

INTERESTING FACTS

The Heathcock was a known hub for local tradesmen in the early 19th century, often cited in local news for its lively atmosphere [cite: 2026-02-03].

HUMAN ELEMENT

John Church, a prominent figure at No. 1, was noted in the 1841 census as the primary resident and manager of the inn's daily operations.
